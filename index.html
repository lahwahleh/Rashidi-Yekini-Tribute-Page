Rashidi Yekini

The man who scored Nigeria's first worldcup goal

Rashidi Yekini celebrates Nigeri’s first worldcup goal

Here's a time line of Rashidi Yekini's life:

  • October 23, 1963 - Rashidi Yekini was born in Kaduna, Nigeria.
  • 1984–98 - During his 14-year career with Nigeria’s Super Eagles, Yekini scored 37 goals in 58 matches, a national record
  • April 6, 1985 - Rashidi Yekini scores his first senior international goal for Nigeria in a 1986 FIFA World Cup qualification match against Kenya. Nigeria won the match 3-0.
  • 1988 - He participated at the Olympics in Seoul , South Korea
  • 1992 – 1993 - He netted a career-best of 34 goals in 32 games to help the Sadinos promote from the second division in Portugal
  • 1993 - Yekini was named African Footballer of the Year.
  • 1993–94 - Become the Primeira Liga's (Portuguese League) top scorer after scoring 21 goals Vitória de Setúba
  • 1994 - Yekini helped the Super Eagles win the 1994 Africa Cup of Nations where he also topped the goal charts and was named best player of the competition.
  • June 21, 1994 - He scored Nigeria’s first-ever goal (against Bulgaria) in a FIFA World Cup tournament. Nigeria won the match 3-0. His celebration (pictured above) after scoring, crying while holding the goal's net, became one of the iconic images of the tournament
  • June 1998 - He also participated in the 1998 World Cup but failed to score.
  • 2003 - At the age of 39, he returned to the Nigerian championship with Julius Berger FC.
  • 2005 - 41-year-old Yekini made a short comeback, moving alongside former national teammate Mobi Oparaku to Gateway United FC.
  • In 2011 - News media in Nigeria begun issuing reports of his failing health, and he was said to suffer from bipolar disorder, depression and some other undisclosed psychiatric condition
  • May 4, 2012 - Rashidi Yekini died aged 48 in Ibadan, Nigeria
